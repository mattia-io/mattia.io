Work Experience

Ever since graduating with merits from London College of Communication (LCC) I have been on a hunt for a stimulating studio to work for, however job listings are so dull these days ( chat app here, fashion e-shop there, next cooking start-up thingy). The scarcity of intriguing job opportunities meant that I have freelanced for the past 2 years.

During this time I was glad to support people I value through my work. Currently I am developing the website for Precious Plastic, an incredible project by Dave Hakkens (launch mid Jan). For the past 3 years I have also worked as freelance writer for Daily Inspiration, with little less than a million readers. Here you can check my full CV.

Now, I am convinced I want to work for NNN and more specifically on the Eco Coin project-  we had actually conceptualized something similar at uni. Obviously any other work required from NNN would be happily welcomed.  

My approach

I like to think of my practice as an exploration of socio, ideological, economic, cultural or ethical issue within the context of everyday life. I've worked on projects about technology, food waste, democracy (or what is left of it), sustainability, vision, semiotic, social equality, possible futures, pollution and freedom of speech. 

My academic projects often represented an intellectual derivé whose ambitions were to pose questions, encourage thoughts, expose assumptions, provoke action, spark debate, trigger motion, raise awareness, offer new perspectives, and ultimately inspire. My professional career somehow failed to foster these aspects and it is exactly this that I am searching within NNN- an environment to explore daring ideas.

 I consider technology as something to exploit and study rather than praise. As a maker I'm keen to know how it function, understand its potentials and relation to society, but there is not any extra level of emotional glorification to it.

Self Veneration

We live in a world where ego and self image are glorified to the extreme. St Leonard Church in Shoreditch was the backdrop for this interactive installation. As the user kneeled on the church's kneeler, a pressure sensor would activate a god-like spotlight and a live streamed video projection of the user just in front of her/him. The scene would then see a user kneeling, praying to their own image with a holy choir in the background.  
Self venerating themselves. 

YAPI

This interactive installation, aims to be a bridge between the digital and physical worlds. The physical world is represented by a geodesic structure resembling the globe in all its immensity, the digital side of our reality is represented by a continuous stream of videos projected onto the outside of the structure. Exploiting the YouTube API, the installation randomly selects 15 videos from all the content uploaded on Youtube in the 5 previous minutes.
Exhibited at Fascinate Conference and Jordan/Seydoux gallery in Berlin.

This is me, myself, Mattia.
This portrait is the most analogue way I found to avoid digital face recognition.

I am a creative from Florence, trained in London, living in Berlin, working everywhere. A digital gypsy of sorts.

Formally educated as an interaction designer at London College of Communication- I now craft websites, create interactive installations, write and try to exercise daily with arguable results.
When I'm not dealing with pixels, motors, lasers or valves I craft handmade caps. I also have a shady past as a practicing chemist but... Ok, let's move on!

I make hats to get ready for the impending baldness and to personally support and contribute to craftsmanship in a time of mass-produced mediocrity. Society deserves better products; better stories.

I provide top class digital consultancy, strategy and development to small businesses, no profits, blogs, NGOs, craftsmen, shops, restaurants and anyone wishing to become more visible, competitive and robust in the digital jungle.

I exploit years worth of design research, UX understanding, behavioral science and content strategy to get businesses to the outstanding industry standard required. 

I believe that happiness is to be found in the process of getting to something, not the actual thing. As such I am constantly submerged in multiple creative projects around the world. Sometimes for $, some other time for fun, virtue or ethic integrity. 

After graduating with merit from BA(hons) in Interaction Design, I have worked for clients you might define as noteworthy. I also have worked for people I consider awesome, influential friends, great makers and up and coming pop bands.

Often, I also work as a design critic. I write about design, art, society and everything in between for Daily Inspiration. Over a million readers spent hours reading my recent musings.

In September 2013 I stopped flying for environmental reasons. If that sort of thing floats your boat you can read about it here.

I am lazily involved in all the social hypocrisies of our times.
